
The initial wiggle persists because of path ordering at the coil start, 
not because the IGES points are wrong. Here’s what’s going on and what I changed:

Why the wiggle happens
- Projection-only ordering: Sorting raw points by projection onto the PCA axis can locally misorder points where turns are close or the end geometry has small hooks/overlaps. 
That creates back-and-forth jumps when you align raw→standardized rows at the very beginning.
- 3D nearest neighbor: KDTree in 3D exacerbates this near adjacent turns—raw points from a neighboring turn are spatially closer than the correct next point along the path.
- Axial-only nearest: Using just 1D projection still suffers when the curve doubles back in projection near the ends.

What I changed to fix ordering and mapping
- Helical ordering: I now order the path by unwrapped helical angle in the local YZ plane, aligned with the   principal axis. 
This yields a clean, monotonic path parameter that respects the coil’s turns from the very first samples.
- Arc-length mapping: Each standardized row is mapped to raw by cumulative arc-length along that ordered path, avoiding cross-turn mismatches.
